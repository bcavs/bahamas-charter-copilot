# Go-To-Market

## Validation Objective

Before building deep integrations, validate that charter operators care about faster lead conversion enough to pay.

The key question:

> Would a captain pay for a tool that helps them respond to WhatsApp inquiries faster and send better quotes?

## First Customer Profile

Best early prospects:

- Bahamas-based charter operators
- owner-operated or small fleet
- uses WhatsApp publicly
- has Instagram or website lead flow
- posts actively
- advertises custom trips or private charters
- lacks polished online booking flow

Avoid first:

- large polished operators with advanced booking systems
- operators that only sell through major marketplaces
- operators with no visible inbound demand

## First Outreach Angle

Lead with revenue and speed, not AI.

Example:

> I am building a simple WhatsApp assistant for charter captains. It helps turn customer messages into clean replies and quotes in under a minute, so you do not lose leads while you are on the water. If I used a few real inquiries from your business, could I show you what it would have replied?

## First Demo Format

Ask for 3 real anonymized inquiries.

Demo:

1. Paste each inquiry.
2. Show extracted lead details.
3. Show missing questions.
4. Show suggested reply.
5. Show quote format.
6. Ask whether they would have sent it.

The best validation signal is not praise. It is the operator saying:

- "Can I use this?"
- "Can it work with my trips?"
- "How much?"
- "Can it follow up for me?"

## Pricing Hypothesis

Early pricing should be simple.

Possible tiers:

- Starter: $49/month for reply drafting and lead tracking
- Pro: $149/month for saved trip menu, follow-up reminders, and quote links
- Concierge: $299/month plus setup for operators who want Ben to configure the system

Potential setup fee:

- $250 to $750 for initial operator setup, trip menu cleanup, quote templates, and policy configuration

Pricing should be tested in calls. Do not over-optimize before real operator feedback.

## Sales Sequence

1. Build interactive demo.
2. Identify 25 operators. _(Running list: [`docs/leads/`](leads/) — 38 prospects compiled, owner-operated WhatsApp/phone bookers prioritized.)_
3. Capture public info: WhatsApp number, Instagram, website, island, trip types.
4. Reach out to 10 with a specific, non-generic message.
5. Offer to demo with their real inquiries.
6. Ask for willingness to pay.
7. Convert 1 to 3 design partners.

## Design Partner Offer

Possible offer:

- free setup
- discounted monthly price for first 3 months
- direct influence on product
- in exchange for weekly feedback and real usage

Design partner criteria:

- has recurring inbound leads
- responds on WhatsApp
- willing to share real inquiry examples
- willing to say what is wrong
- has potential to introduce other operators

## Expansion Path

### Operator Network

Once several operators use the system, the product accumulates structured supply:

- trips
- routes
- prices
- policies
- destinations
- photos
- service quality signals

### Boutique Hotels

Hotel pitch later:

> Give your guests trusted charter options without your concierge team manually texting every operator.

Hotel product later:

- preferred activity menu
- concierge quote requests
- referral tracking
- vetted operator list

### Vacation Rental Managers

Rental manager pitch later:

> Add a guest activity layer powered by local operators already using the platform.

This works better after the operator network exists.

## Travel Justification

The business creates legitimate reasons for Ben to travel to the Bahamas:

- meet operators
- onboard trips and routes
- take photos/videos
- understand guest pickup logistics
- build hotel relationships
- test curated packages

The travel should be tied to revenue-generating field work, not just inspiration.

## Early Metrics

Track:

- number of operators contacted
- number of demo calls booked
- number of real inquiries processed
- operator rating of draft usefulness
- reply time reduction
- quoted leads
- won leads
- willingness to pay
- monthly recurring revenue

## Risks

### Manual MVP Feels Too Manual

Mitigation: first demo must feel automated even if implementation is lightweight.

### WhatsApp API Friction

Mitigation: start with mobile web companion and copy/open-in-WhatsApp. Add Business Platform integration later.

### Captains Distrust Automation

Mitigation: copilot-first approval model.

### Operators Do Not Pay For Software

Mitigation: sell revenue recovery and professional quoting, not software.

### Marketplace Temptation

Mitigation: do not build demand aggregation until operator supply and workflow usage exist.
